Humans are extremely fast learners and can learn and apply novel concepts from just one example and a short Q&A session with their teacher. They can paraphrase these concepts and recognize them even if they are slightly modified. I am deeply interested in identifying mathematical patterns in these abilities, and then reconstructing them in robots and artificial agents.

Research Questions:
  1. How should robots combine information from all their senses (visual, auditory etc.) to perceive reality?
  2. How should multi-sensory information be represented, mathematically, so that important ideas can be abstracted, generalized and re-applied in different situations?
  3. What is mathematical structure of a human imagination and what type of computational architecture is needed to allow robots to imagine and reason through alternative realities?
Collaborations, anyone?

My research is inherently multi-disciplinary and I'm always looking for collaborators who are driven, like I am, to work together on questions about human cognition and artificial intelligence.

Here are some topics I am currently exploring:

  1. Mathematics - Dempster-Shafer Theory, Uncertain Logic, Event Calculus
  2. Computer Science - Artificial Intelligence (AI), Knowledge Representation, Answer Set Programming
  3. Cognitive Science/Psychology - Learning Strategies, Pattern Recognition, Decision-Making
  4. Philosophy - Causality, Affordances, Imagination
  5. Neuroscience - Perception, Ideasthesia and Synesthesia
