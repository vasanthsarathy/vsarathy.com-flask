Vasanth Sarathy is a PhD student, jointly in Computer Science and Cognitive Science at Tufts University. Vasanth grew up in Muscat, Oman and moved to the United States for college. After graduating summa cum laude in Electrical Engineering from the University of Arkansas in 2003, Vasanth studied at MIT, where he conducted research in the mathematical modeling of printed circuit board insulation at the Laboratory for Electromagnetic and Electronic Systems (LEES @ RLE). He obtained a Masters Degree in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science at MIT in 2005. From 2005 to 2013, Vasanth worked as an Intellectual Property Lawyer at Ropes & Gray, during which time he concurrently obtained a JD from Boston University School of Law. His love for science, math, the human thought process and creativity pulled him back into the academic world.

Vasanth loves to cartoon and some of his work can be seen on Legally Drawn, cartoons poking fun at law and lawyers. In addition to cartooning, Vasanth has pursued entrepreneurial ventures to explore various sides of the human thought process. For example, he designed MetaMoo, a web app to explore ideas on how the human brain organizes information and finds creative connection.
